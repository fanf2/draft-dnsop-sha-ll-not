1.  Introduction

1.1.  Timeline

   o  2005: Theoretical 2^63 attack on SHA-1 by Wang Xiaoyun et al.

   o  2006: NIST starts to deprecate SHA-1
      https://csrc.nist.gov/News/2006/NIST-Comments-on-Cryptanalytic-
      Attacks-on-SHA-1

   o  2010: DNS root zone signed with RSASHA256
      https://www.root-dnssec.org/

   o  2011: NIST formally deprecates SHA-1 for digital signatures, and
      disallows it after 2013
      https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/Legacy/SP/
      nistspecialpublication800-131a.pdf

   o  2013: IETF recommends RSASHA1 for use in DNSSEC [RFC6944]

   o  2014: CA/Browser forum sunsets SHA-1 in X.509 WebPKI certificates
      after 2015
      https://cabforum.org/2014/10/16/ballot-118-sha-1-sunset/

   o  2015: SHAppening free-start collision demonstrated in SHA-1
      https://sites.google.com/site/itstheshappening/

   o  2017: SHAttered classical collision demonstrated in SHA-1
      https://shattered.io/

   o  2019: IETF deprecates SHA-1 for use in DNSSEC [RFC8624]

   o  2020: SHAmbles chosen-prefix collision demonstrated in SHA-1
      https://sha-mbles.github.io/
